Housing First

What is Housing First?

The Housing First principle views housing as a fundamental right and is an effective and evidence-based tool for preventing and ending homelessness for the most vulnerable groups. Because it addresses the issue of homelessness with a key response — housing and support services.

How it works

This is affordable rental housing with professional support, which is provided for the Košice-Prešov pilot project by the non-profit organization Everyone for the Family, but the professional multidisciplinary team adapts its services to clients according to their needs, tailor-made and sensitive to their circumstances, providing services to them at home. In addition to a social worker, the team also includes a health worker, psychologist or lawyer. Clients pay regular rent for their housing and from the first moment they become a legitimate part of the community in the given location.

The condition for participation in the project for families is, in addition to paying rent, a commitment to be a good neighbor and receiving professional support. They gain a home, privacy, and the opportunity to truly change their life situation.

Rent a home

The family belongs to the home. Rent it to us.

Do you have a vacant apartment and are you wondering how to rent it out safely? Cooperate with us in the Housing First program and help a family in housing need obtain stable housing. The DEDO Foundation will provide professional guidance to tenants, regular communication and support throughout the entire rental period.

Our social services combine intensive case management with community-based support. Each family receives a tailored plan addressing financial literacy, parenting, education, and social integration.

schedule Average 100+ counseling hours per family annually

Comprehensive field social services

We were the first in Slovakia to start an innovative service of accompanying and supporting families in housing need, for whom we end the situation of homelessness by enabling them to live in affordable housing and long-term support aimed at maintaining housing.

We work in the field — in Košice and Prešov we search for families with children in housing need, identify their needs and respond to them with professional help, the basis of which is housing and integrated health and social services.

Multidisciplinary teams are composed of social workers, psychologists, lawyers, peer workers and work based on the individual needs of families. The intensity and frequency of visits is adjusted to the dynamics of the families. Visits to the families concerned take place at their homes.

We apply the Housing First and Trauma Informed Care approaches.

Work integration

Tailoring Workshop, as a social enterprise, where unique fashion bags and accessories are created from unnecessary banners, and the Supported Employment Agency — APZ are 2 basic pillars of work inclusion.

Tailoring Workshop creates jobs and a fair, safe working environment, and APZ seeks opportunities on the open labor market.

Professional job coaches work with clients individually, applying the Individual Placement and Support — IPS approach, the basic principles of which help people regain self-worth and fulfill their career ambitions.

Health is inseparable from housing stability. Our integrated model co-locates health workers within the Housing First team, ensuring continuous care from street outreach through sustained independent living.

Integrated health services

In extreme poverty, access to healthcare is often unavailable. That is why we are creating a model of integrated health-social support — we provide treatment and care, accompany clients to specialists, provide psychological assistance and remove practical barriers such as fees or bureaucracy.

A general practitioner for adults, a psychiatrist, a psychologist, and trained medical staff provide healthcare to vulnerable groups of the population as part of a multidisciplinary team, both in outpatient clinics and in the field, wherever homeless people live.

We are bringing a unique comprehensive care model in Central Europe to Košice and, from 2026, to Prešov.

NGOs

Through the PUB Platform and membership in FEANTSA (European Federation of National Organisations Working with the Homeless), we shape homelessness policy from local municipalities to EU institutions, ensuring lived experience informs every strategy.

Advocacy and research

"Our work should not be exceptional, but ordinary."

We work with cities, institutions, experts and communities to bring models of ending homelessness into public policies. Together we create strategy, share data, train workers and bring functional solutions to other cities.

We carry out research activities, obtain data that are the basis for our argumentation.

PUB — Platform for Ending Homelessness

We initiated the creation of the national umbrella organization PUB — Platform for Ending Homelessness. The PUB Platform brings together organizations and experts who believe that homelessness can be ended.

We create a space for collaboration, exchange of experiences and joint advocacy. We support the spread of working models, such as Housing First, and strive for systemic change at the national level.

European partnership

We are members of the European Federation of National Organisations Working with Homeless People (FEANTSA).

As experts, we bring our knowledge and experience to working groups at the local, national and international levels — not only in the field of ending homelessness, but also in the field of social innovation.
